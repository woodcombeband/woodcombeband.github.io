Woodcombe

the mayor of loserville

verse i
you built this town you’re destroying
the one which you claim to hate
the town in whose foundations
your name is there engraved

verse ii
the people act on your teaching
yet you claim to disagree
you come roaring, lashing, seething
when they lift up their works

verse iii
one day they won’t seek you
one day they’ll leave you alone
only then will you find your desire
for their insecure delusions

chorus i
why did you build this town?
from which you live willingly exiled
in your mansion of self righteousness
with your glass cannon temperament

verse iv
why did you build this town?
for which you feel no pride
this town has flourished
become beautiful on its own

verse v
but it’s not exactly what you want
everything’s one inch to the left
which begs the question what if
it was exactly what you wanted?

chorus ii
would you feel satisfied?
would you feel full inside?
no, because you’ve got a hole
with a vacuum at the bottom

verse vi
you’ll never be finished
you’ll always want another town
the population ever growing
and the starvation ever killing

verse vii
why did you build this town?
you have eight more before it
you did not need this town
so why did you build this town?
